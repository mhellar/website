One of the first electronic artworks to feature a live feed in the gallery, Haackeâ€™s News (1969) consists of a real-time printed newsfeed. A pile of paper grows as it spills from the printer on the table onto the floor, and visitors are welcome to pick up portions to read. News was acquired in 2008 by SFMOMA. News was to be re-exhibited at SFMOMA in 2018 for the Nothing Stable Under Heaven exhibition. In preparation for the show, I was asked to re-write the software as the 2008 version had become unable to run on modern computer operating systems. This version was also shown at Artists Respond: American Art and the Vietnam War, 1965-1975, at the Smithsonian American Art Museum in 2019.
