The artist Lynn Hershman Leeson has pioneered themes of media-based technology artwork since the sixties. I developed a working relationship with her while preserving her artificially intelligent artwork, Agent Ruby for the San Francisco Museum of Art in 2009. I then started working with her to write software and help conceive of new works for exhibition. In this article, I explore the relationship between the artist and the technologist to discover what is found there. Insights focus on the perspectives of creation, reflections on the preservation of these works and how at times these themes conflict and inform each other.
